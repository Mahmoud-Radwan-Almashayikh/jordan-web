01 / Wonder of the World

The Rose-Red
City of Petra

Carved directly into rose-red sandstone cliffs more than 2,000 years ago, Petra stands as Jordan's most iconic treasure and one of humanity's greatest architectural achievements. Enter through the Siq — a narrow gorge nearly two kilometres long — and emerge to the breathtaking façade of Al-Khazneh.

02 / Valley of the Moon

Wadi Rum —
Mars on Earth

Vast red sand dunes, ancient petroglyphs, and towering sandstone pillars define one of the world's most dramatic desert landscapes. Wadi Rum served as the backdrop for Lawrence of Arabia and The Martian — because nothing on Earth looks quite like it.

03 / Natural Wonder

Float on Water,
Heal Your Soul

The Dead Sea is the lowest point on Earth — a hypnotic stretch of indigo water so dense with salt that the human body floats effortlessly on its surface. Its mineral-rich mud has been celebrated for therapeutic properties since Cleopatra's time.

05 / Living Heritage

A Culture
Woven in Time

Jordanian culture is a magnificent tapestry of Bedouin traditions, Ottoman heritage, and Pan-Arab warmth. Hospitality here is not a gesture — it is a way of life. A stranger is a guest to be honoured with coffee, dates, and stories that span generations.

Diwaniyyah

The ancient gathering hall where stories, poetry and politics intertwine

Zarb & Mansaf

Communal feasts cooked underground — the ultimate Bedouin welcome

Dabke

The rhythmic folk dance performed at every celebration and wedding
